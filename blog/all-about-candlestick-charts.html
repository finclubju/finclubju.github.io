All About Candlestick Charts

One important consideration in technical analysis is that we rely on the fact that history often repeats itself. It would be wise to examine this idea in more detail at this time as the candlestick patterns are very much dependent on it.
Finance Blog Opinion

Before diving deep into candles lets understand why we need them. So basically there are many types of charts that you will encounter while trading. Some important ones of them are:

1. Line Chart:

Line chart is the simplest form of chart. Figure above shows a line chart. In line chart only the closing price of each day is plotted as against the open, high low and close which is plotted in a bar chart of candlestick chart.

One of the main disadvantage of this type of chart is that it doesn’t give you the detailed information about the price at a particular time frame. Suppose we are on our one hour time frame, let the time be 10 AM to 11 AM. Suppose at 10 AM the price is $100 and at 10:30 AM the price rises to $200 and at 11 AM the price falls to $120. Then a line chart will show value of $120 for the entire 1 hour time frame. This means that a line chart erases most of the details of price movement. Then why do we use it? It shows us the price trend over a time period more easily and erases the small disruptions in price.

2. Bar Chart:

Bar charts are some of the most popular type of charts used in technical analysis. The image above is of a bar chart.

A green bar means that the Final value is greater than the initial value and a red bar means that the final value is less than the initial value. This is the reason that the closing price of a red bar is smaller than its opening price while the closing price of a green bar is greater than its opening price.

The main advantage of using a bar chart over a straight line graph is that it shows the high, low, open and close for each particular time frame which was not present on the line chart.

3. Candle Stick Charting:

Candlestick chart has been around for hundreds of years they are often also referred to as “Japanese candles” because the Japanese would use them to analyse the price of price contracts. These candles to are exactly similar to the bar graph with respect to the highest praise the lowest price of the day the opening price and the closing price.

Types of Candles

Now let’s understand the different types of candles and understand what they want to tell us.

1. Doji Candle

Doji is formed when the opening and closing price of virtual equal. (The open and close ideally should be equal but not necessarily the same). The length of the upper and lower shadows can vary.

Doji represents indecision or tug-of-war between buyers and sellers. The stock was trading higher and lower than the opening level during the session but closed at all very near to the opening. This means neither buyers nor sellers could gain control signaling in decision and that a turning point from the existing trend could be nearing.

Dodji by itself is a neutral pattern . Doji could provide a reversal signal in the in relation to the preceding trend and future price confirmation. Hence when a Doji forms on the chart pay attention to the preceding trends.

Doji in itself is of 3 types:

A. Long-Legged Doji:

Long legged Doji have long upper and lower shadows that are almost equal in length. Such a doji indicates that price moved considerably above and below the open during the session but closed at or very near to the opening price is reflecting a great amount of indecision or confusion in the market.

B. Dragonfly Doji:

Dragonfly Doji is formed when the opening and closing price or at the height of the session and the low price creates a long lower shadow resulting in a candlestick with a long lower shadow, and no upper shadow. It looks like a “T”.

Dragonfly Doji indicates that sellers dominated trading and pushed the price lower in the beginning of session. However, towards the end of the session, buyers re-emerged and drove the price back to the opening level and the session high. This signal is a potential trend reversal. A point to be noted is that the colour of doji candle doesn’t affect its type.

C. Gravestone Doji:

Gravestone Doji is just the opposite of dragonfly Doji. Gravestone Doji is formed when the opening and closing price or at the low of the session and the high price creates along the upper shadow resulting in a candle stick with a long upper shadow and no lower shadow bracket open looks like NTGravestone Doji is just the opposite of dragonfly Doji. Gravestone Doji is formed when the opening and closing price or at the low of the session and the high price creates a long upper shadow resulting in a candlestick with a long upper shadow and no lower shadow (looks like an “T”).

2. Hanging Man

Hanging man is a reversal pattern. It may be formed at the end of an uptrend or downtrend or at a resistance or support reversing the ongoing trend.

For a Bearish hanging man to form the price must first trade much higher then where it opened and then it drops to close near its low at the end of the day. The long upper shadow formed shows some indications that the buyers might have started to step in.although the sellers managed to regain control and drive the price lower at the close, The appearance of buying pressure gives some warning.

For a bullish hanging man to form the price must first trade much lower than where it opened and then it rallies to close near its highs at the end of the day the long lower shadow formed shows some indications that the selling pressure might just begin.

3. Marubozu Candle

Marubozu is the name of a Japanese candlesticks formation used in technical analysis to indicate a stock has traded strongly in one direction throughout the session and closed at its high or low price of the day. If we look at the candle clearly then we can see in case of a bullish marubozu prise opened at a point and then it shooted up till the highest point without forming any wicks/shadows. Marubozu candle is a symbol of dominance on the chart.

There are many more candlestick pattern that you will see on charts, But the ones that I mentioned above are the most fundamental and important ones.
