Technical Indicators

The technical indicator is a technical tool that helps you analyze stock price movements, whether the ongoing trend continues or reverses. This helps traders decide whether to enter or exit a particular stock.
Finance Blog Opinion

For beginners, the first question that might come in your mind is “What are Indicators?” Does it indicate something in market? Well, the answer is YES!! In Financial Markets be it Stocks, Forex, Bonds, Commodities, Cryptocurrencies, Ever Trader is trying to find an edge over the market by which he/she will be profitable. They apply various techniques to try to be on the better side. One of the techniques that they use is “Indicators”.

Let’s what define the term indicators in the world of Technical Analysis!

An indicator is a mathematical calculation that can be used with the stock’s price and/or volume to help make investment choices. The end result is a value that’s used to anticipate future changes in price. There are two types of indicators: leading and lagging.

Now a question might come into your mind, If an instrument such as “Indicator” is present in market which will indicate what the chart is going to do the very next moment, then why do 90% of traders fail in the market?

well, your question is a genuine one, Indicators do indicate what the price is going to do the very next moment! but will it do for 100% of the time? No!

Indicator use the data derived from other sources such as Price, Open Interest, Volume etc. i.e. it is not a fundamental tool, but a derived tool. Initially changes in price occurs and then after some lag, changes are seen on the indicator. So, whatever seems to be too easy isn’t so easy.

If indicators are not a fundamental tool then why do we use it? we use it because when indicators are used with other tools such as Price Action, Order Blocks, Volume, Magic starts to happen.

There are Thousands if not Lakhs of indicators present in the market. Do we need to learn all of them? NO. There are a couple of famous indicators which many traders use. I will be discussing about them in depth now:

1. Relative Strength Index (RSI)

The relative strength index (RSI) is a momentum indicator used in technical analysis that measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions in the price of a stock or other asset. The RSI is displayed as an oscillator (a line graph that moves between two extremes) and can have a reading from 0 to 100. The indicator was originally developed by J. Welles Wilder Jr. and introduced in his seminal 1978 book, “New Concepts in Technical Trading Systems.”

RSI can be imagined similar to a Barometer, It is basically a scale that runs from 0 to 100. 40 is the lower limit and 60 is the upper limit (Traditionally it is 30 and 70). When the indicator lies below 40, it is in an oversold region and now people can start buying that asset. When indicator lies above 60, it is in overbought region and most people have made their money on that asset and now they are selling.

E.g. Reliance Industries

This is chart of Reliance Industries NSE: RELIANCE on Daily Timeframe. Every time RSI was above the purple band (>60), The market saw a fall while, every time it was below the lower band (<40) market saw a pump.

2. Moving Averages (MA)

A moving average is a technical indicator that combines price points of an instrument over a specified time frame, and divides by the number of data points, to give you a single trend line. It is popular amongst traders because it can help to determine the direction of the current trend, while lessening the impact of random price spikes. A moving average will enable you to examine the levels of support and resistance, by analysing the previous movement of an asset’s price. It is a measure of change that trails the previous price action of an asset, assessing the history of market movements to determine possible future patterns. A moving average is primarily a lagging indicator, which makes it one of the most popular tools for technical analysis.

Calculating an MA requires a certain amount of data, which can be a large quantity depending on the length of the moving average. For instance, a ten-day MA will require ten days of data, while a one-year MA will require 365 days’ worth. A 200- day period is a very commonly used timeframe for MA. The indicator is described as ‘moving’ because the introduction of new figures will replace old data points and ‘move’ the line on the chart.

Some popular Moving averages are 20, 50 and 200. 20 being the most local is important for small moves and 200 being a large range can be important support/resistance for big moves and 50 being the intermediate. In my chart, the colour coding is as follows:

Red: 50 Moving Average.

3. Average Directional Index (ADX)

ADX stands for Average Directional Movement Index and can be used to help measure the overall strength of a trend. The ADX indicator is an average of expanding price range values. The ADX is a component of the Directional Movement System developed by Welles Wilder. This system attempts to measure the strength of price movement in positive and negative direction using the DMI+ and DMIindicators along with the ADX.

ADX indicator is a symbol of strength, It doesn’t suggests anything about the direction of the market, but what is suggests is the strength with which the market is moving. If the strength is high, the move will continue else the move will fail.

ADX value >25 means strong strength while ADX<20 means no strength in market.
E.g. Reliance Industries

4. Bollinger Bands

Bollinger Bands are a type of price envelope developed by John Bollinger (Price envelopes define upper and lower price range levels.) Bollinger Bands are envelopes plotted at a standard deviation level above and below a simple moving average of the price. Because the distance of the bands is based on standard deviation, they adjust to volatility swings in the underlying price.

Bollinger Bands use 2 parameters, Period and Standard Deviations. The default values are 20 for period, and 2 for standard deviations, although you may customize the combinations.

Bollinger bands help determine whether prices are high or low on a relative basis. They are used in pairs, both upper and lower bands and in conjunction with a moving average. Further, the pair of bands is not intended to be used on its own. Use the pair to confirm signals given with other indicators.

In simple words, the indicator tells us to sell the asset when it touches the upper band while buy when it touches the lower band

E.g. Reliance Industries

5. MACD

The Moving Average Convergence/Divergence indicator is a momentum oscillator primarily used to trade trends. Although it is an oscillator, it is not typically used to identify over bought or oversold conditions. It appears on the chart as two lines which oscillate without boundaries. The crossover of the two lines give trading signals similar to a two moving average system.

In simple words, MACD when is above zero, it is giving a buying signal while when it is lower than zero, it is indicating to sell

E.g. GAIL India
